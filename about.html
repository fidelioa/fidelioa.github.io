Most of these fragments are things I found myself returning to years later. This space operates less like a traditional repository and more like an archive of echoes—lines, frames, and verses that refused to leave my mind after the pages were closed or the projection cut to black. In the digital rush, things are too easily lost. This serves as a quiet shelter for whatever lingers.

The collection here builds itself without real agenda or rigid structure. It is a slow gathering of personal maps, charting how specific narratives change shapes upon a second or third visit. By sorting through these remnants in the dark, the hope isn't to over-analyze or review the work, but rather to honor the specific artistic debris that leaves a mark long after the first encounter.

Books, cinema, and whatever survives a second reading.

"The rest is silence."

Most of these fragments are things I found myself returning to years later. This site operates less like a structured portfolio and more like a sensory repository—a personal archive of lines, scenes, and verses that flatly refused to leave my head. We consume so much fleeting art in the modern noise that the things which actually manage to stick around deserve their own quiet home in the dark.

I find a strange comfort in keeping raw textual leftovers and cinematic artifacts exactly as they were discovered. If you look closely through the pages, there is an accidental map here of everything that has silently shaped how I see things. No commentary, no heavy analytical deconstruction—just the echoes that survived the second reading.

Books, cinema, and whatever manages to haunt its way into a permanent archive.

"The rest is silence."
